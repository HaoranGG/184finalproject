Haoran Guo, Steven Cen, Philip Green



Our project aims to make a fluid dynamics simulation that visualizes music. We will do a 2D grid based simulation as suggested on one of the project ideas, and sync up its movement to music signals and features.

Problem

Fluid dynamics is an important branch of science that lets us study the mechanics behind situations that range from ocean waves to blood circulation. Visualization is vital to helping us understand and better describe pretty much anything, so a fluid dynamics simulation is certainly valuable. In this project we’d like to add a musical flare since we think artistic value is significant value as well. The most challenging part will probably be performing the physics calculations and creating the simulation from the results properly. We’ll be looking into the GLSL fragment shaders as mentioned and take inspiration from other fluid dynamic simulations and music visualizations online.

Goals and Deliverables

We will create a visualization software that will generate videos to music of the team members’ choice. This software will take basic features from music such as frequency, signal strength as inputs to equations governing fluid dynamics, particularly the parameters of the Navier-Stokes equations, and other values such as vorticity. We also should extend Navier-Stokes in creative ways, such as incorporating extra external forces (such as gravity, buoyancy) to better reflect the music input. Along the same line, we will measure the success of these outputs through the intuition of how we expect the fluid to behave given a certain song and; eg. a loud song with a fast tempo should render a clip that appears more energetic and lively. This project would require non-trivial time dealing with audio signal processing and feature extraction in music but will not be the focus in research and work time.

We are ideally looking to make a deliverable in which users can upload music and be able to visualize it given some customizable parameters. These parameters could color palette choices. If given enough time, we could even make it so the visualizer responds to audio real time from the user’s microphone. To make the visualizer look “nicer” and more of what the team members are personally looking for, we should implement some heuristics and/or machine learning algorithms to manipulate the fluid dynamics in a specific way. We also have room to go above and beyond in thinking of extra creative extensions of Navier-Stokes beyond extra forces, as well as a deeper exploration into feature extraction .
